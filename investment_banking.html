Investment Banking

Let's learn about investment banking with Larry

Larry’s Lemonade: Time for the Big Bucks

Larry’s business has been expanding extremely fast. He now has lemonade stands in almost 30 states and 120 cities. Larry has been thinking of what his next steps are, and he is thinking about taking his company public. He gives his friend Ivan a call. Ivan is an investment banker at one of the nation’s largest banks, Goldman Suisse. Ivan helps Larry by filing for an IPO (initial public offering) to issue new shares of stock. What this means is that Larry will sell part of his equity (ownership) in the company to the public, in the form of tiny pieces (stock shares). Anyone can buy or sell those shares, as facilitated by traders on a stock exchange like the NYSE. After some time, Ivan notifies Larry that his company is worth $50 million! The company’s worth is divided into one million shares, each worth $50 (the IPO). Larry retains 51% of those shares so that he becomes the majority shareholder and thereby retains the decision-making authority.

Investment Banking in a Nutshell

    Investment bankers:
  • Advise companies that are looking to go public or get involved in M&A
  • Connect the bank’s wealthy investor clients with emerging companies that need funds and align with their investment interests
  • Help make deals happen
  • Assist companies who lack the time and/or expertise for these kinds of transactions

Why Engineers in IB

Investment banking is notorious for its long work weeks (80-90 hours per week). Due to the daunting rigor and length of collegiate engineering programs, engineers have a relatively strong work ethic and stamina. As in many other facets of business, strong analytical skills are cherished in investment banking.
