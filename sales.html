Larry’s Lemonade: Sami Helps Out

Larry’s business is doing really well! However, he has had a few issues after hiring the 14-year-old neighbor to run his stand (she keeps tweeting and Snapchatting her boyfriend instead of helping customers). He lets her go, and decides to hire on Sami as the Lemonade VP of Sales. Sami understands that in order for Larry’s Lemonade to grow, she needs to sell more lemonade. She does this in several ways. She offers free samples of the flavored lemonade to her everyday customers, hoping to “upsell” them to the more expensive but better-tasting drinks. She goes out on the street and spreads the word about the wonderful lemonade that's inside! Sami tells them that it is the best lemonade they will ever taste, and she even offers them a money-back guarantee if they don’t agree. Her upbeat personality and ability to carry on a conversation with anyone proves to be a valuable asset to Larry. Ever since he brought Sami onboard, his lemonade sales have increased by 15%.

Sales in a Nutshell

Salespeople are focused on exchanging their products or service for money Sales is the heart of business; if you can’t sell anything, you can’t make any money Salespeople are generally paid on commission, meaning that they get a percentage of the money made in a sale The two most popular types of sales are business-to-business (B2B) or business-to-consumer (B2C)

Why Engineers in Sales?

“If you want to be successful in business, you HAVE to be successful in sales.” -Every successful business person I have ever met While sales gets a bad rep, especially in Provo ("Wanna make more than your dad this summer, bro?"), it is a crucial skill that can be a powerful arrow in your quiver. Sales is about creating value in the mind of the customer. They need to value your product at a higher price than what you are asking for. It is easy to understand, but hard to master. We are surrounded by sales! Sami had to SELL the idea of an ice cold lemonade to hot and sweaty customers. Engineers and academics have to SELL their managers on the idea on giving them hundreds of thousands of dollars to spend on research and development. Mom and Dad have to SELL the idea to their kids that practicing the piano and doing their homework will pay off in the future. I have to SELL my professor on the idea that he should drop the second midterm that I may or may not have bombed. See where I’m getting at? Sales is everywhere.
