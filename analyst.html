Analyst

Let's learn about being an analyst with Larry

Larry's Lemonade: Buying Bob's Beverages

Larry is up against some stiff competition in the drink industry! Bob’s Beverages has been stealing a lot of Larry’s market share, and Larry is looking for ways to cope. One of the options he is considering is buying Bob’s Beverages and growing inorganically. He gives his management consulting friend Carly a call, and Carly sends him an analyst named Aaron. Aaron is an analyst who specializes in mergers and acquisitions. His job is to provide Larry with a recommendation of whether or not to buy Bob’s Beverages and provide the evidence behind the recommendation. Aaron looks into multiple aspects of Bob’s Beverages, including his product mix and quality, the caliber of the current employees and management, profitability, and the state of the drink market. He searches and compiles numbers for a few weeks, and then sets up a meeting with Larry. While Bob sells lemonade, he specializes in flavored colas and is extremely profitable. Taking the data and using algorithms including current market trends, Larry finds out that if he were to buy Bob’s Beverages, he would get a return on his investment in less than 2 years and would have over 80% of the drink market!

Analyst in a Nutshell

  • Analysts deal with raw data and turn it into useful information that can be used to make decisions
  • They can either look at data for another company to determine the health of the company (usually called "due diligence"), or they can work in-house to help with strategic planning

Why Engineers as Analysts

Number crunching and data galore. Need I say more?
An analyst specializes in gathering data, simplifying it to something useful, and drawing conclusions. With a technical background, engineers are really good at finding data, and then figuring out which data is important. Numbers, equations, information, and an attention to detail are especially relevant.
