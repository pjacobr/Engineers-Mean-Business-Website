Operations

Let's learn about operations with Larry

Larry’s Lemonade: Growing and Growing

Larry has a few stands open, and he plans on opening a few more. However, as he expands, there are more moving parts and more problems he needs to solve. He finds it is difficult to work on the high-level business plan and keep up with the day-to-day business operations. He hires Oliver to help him with all the day-to-day work. Oliver is put in as the operations manager. He oversees all the daily workings and issues to make sure things are running smoothly. He makes sure the suppliers are on time in their deliveries. He makes sure that production is steady at all of his stores, and helps store managers make and reach their goals for their production. He ensures that everything is running smoothly, efficiently, and financial sound. He does his best to make improvements where he can.

Operations in a Nutshell

  • Operation managers are almost like little CEOs in their own sphere
  • They make sure that all business functions are working correctly and efficiently

Why engineers in Operations?

Most business operations, especially one where a product is being made, are extremely complex. Can you imagine trying to explain why a complex piece of manufacturing equipment isn’t working to someone with a finance background? Engineers are able to understand the basic concepts of certain problems, which allows them to find solutions. While a normal operations manager has to call in outside engineers to fix his problems, someone with an engineering background is able to fix them on his own.
